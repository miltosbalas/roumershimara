The Setting

Himarë: where the mountains fall into the Ionian

Himarë is a sun-bleached town on Albania's southern coast, where olive groves and goat trails on Mount Çikë drop almost straight into water so clear you can count the pebbles three metres down. Settled by Greek colonists in antiquity and later home to a Byzantine hilltop castle, it's been a crossroads of Greek, Ottoman and Albanian life for over two thousand years.

Today it's quietly become one of the Mediterranean's most talked-about secrets: the same turquoise water as the Greek islands across the strait, a fraction of the crowds, and a castle-topped old town where stone houses still climb the hillside exactly as they did a century ago.

Fun fact: on a clear day you can see the Greek island of Corfu from the hills above Himarë — that's how close the two coastlines really are.
